Important Notes and Caveats

This online tool is less flexible than the full analysis presented in our paper. It cannot accept arbitrary input photometry, parallax, rotational velocity, and extinction. This is due to the large size of the grid of the stellar models when including rotation. If you would like to compute posterior probability distributions with parameters of your choice (including reddening), please e-mail tbrandt-at-ias-dot-edu. The server here regularly deletes probability distributions it has computed; we will not see your queries.

Assumptions of the Online Tool's Analysis

  • The Hipparcos parallaxes are correct. This is demonstrably untrue in the case of the Pleiades, but should be a good assumption for nearby stars.
  • The additional systematic uncertainties that we add are sufficient to capture systematics in the stellar models.
  • Reddening is negligible. A future version of this calculator will hopefully allow for user-specified reddening. In the meantime, e-mail tbrandt-at-ias-dot-edu for an analysis with arbitrary parameters.
  • Stellar rotation and evolution have had little effect on the surface abundances. The failure of this assumption could significantly impact the results for chemically peculiar stars.
